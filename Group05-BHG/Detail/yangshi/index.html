Introduction

CCTV Headquarters is located at No. 32 Dongsanhuan Zhong Lu, Chaoyang District, Beijing, which is located in the central business district of Beijing (CBD) and next to Beijing International Trade Building. The park consists of three buildings: the CCTV headquarters building (main building) in the southwest, the TV culture center (north wing building) in the northwest, and the energy service center in the northeast corner

CCTV Headquarters

Main scenes

The park consists of three buildings, the CCTV Headquarters Building (the main building) in the southwest, the TV Culture Center (the north wing building) in the northwest and the energy service center in the northeast. The park covers a total area of 187,000 square meters, and the total construction area is about 550,000 square meters.

The CCTV Headquarters Building (Main Building) consists of two towers of 52 stories, 234 meters high and 44 stories, 194 meters high, with 10 floors of podium buildings, which are intersected by large cantilevered steel structures of 14 stories and 18,000 tons, extending at 162 meters in height, with a total steel capacity of 140,000 tons. The two towers are tilted 6 degrees in both directions. Connected by an "L" shaped cantilever structure above 163 meters, the glass facade of the building's exterior is composed of a strong irregular geometric pattern. The main building is divided into five areas: administrative management area, comprehensive business area, news production and broadcasting area, broadcast area and program production area, which will be partially open to the public.

CCTV Television Culture Center (North Wing Building) is a public cultural facility of CCTV facing the society and a window for external publicity and cultural exchange. By five-star hotels, recording studio, digital cinema, stage tower, after the stage, theatre stands, video room, and the exhibition hall, a total construction area of 103648 square meters, four or five layers of the building will house the hotel lobby and restaurants, shops, swimming pool and other public places, the lobby upper north and south sides of 300 guest rooms through into the atrium, the top floor for the hotel's restaurant

Architectural Features

The CCTV headquarters building was designed by Rem Koolhaas and Ole Scheuren, led by the Office for Metropolitan Architecture (OMA). The surface of CCTV headquarters building is composed of glass curtain wall with irregular geometric pattern, which has great visual impact. Glass curtain wall not only has the advantages of light weight, convenient construction, its reflective performance can make the building and the surrounding scenery organically combined together

On August 8, 2008, CCTV set up the Beijing Olympic Games Broadcast Center in the headquarters building of CCTV.
On January 24, 2014, the fire department of Beijing Municipal Public Security Bureau and CCTV jointly held an experiential fire training and evacuation drill in the CCTV headquarters building.
On April 10, 2015, the CCTV headquarters building was cleaned for the first time since its completion, which took 40 days
