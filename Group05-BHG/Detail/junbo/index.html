Introduction

The Military Museum of the Chinese People's Revolution is the only large-scale comprehensive military history museum in China, covering an area of more than 80,000 square meters, with a building area of more than 60,000 square meters and an exhibition area of more than 40,000 square meters. The main building is 94.7 meters high, with 7 floors in the center and 4 floors on both sides. By the end of 2012, the museum has 22 exhibition halls and 2 display squares. Junbo has a collection of more than 340,000 cultural relics and collections.

Military Museum

Main scenes

The Modern War Pavilion began to prepare in 1984 and was officially opened in 1988. It was revised and adjusted in 1998, and reopened in 1999. In May 2012, the museum was closed due to the reinforcement and renovation project of the Military Museum Exhibition Building. The Museum of Modern War is located on the west side of the 4th floor of the original exhibition building of the Military Museum of the Chinese People's Revolution. It covers an area of 1,300 square meters and displays more than 500 pieces of cultural relics, historical photos, restored models, sand tables, charts, wax figures and landscape.

The Museum of Anti-Japanese War is located on the second floor of the former exhibition building of the Military Museum of the Chinese People's Revolution. The museum mainly displays the historical process of the whole nation's war of resistance against Japanese aggression from July 1937 to August 1945 under the banner of the anti-Japanese national united front advocated by the Communist Party of China and based on the cooperation between the Kuomintang and the Communist Party. [17] "Anti-Japanese War Museum" exhibition area of 1650 square meters. The number of cultural relics is 810 pieces, 11 works of art.

The National Liberation War Museum is located on the east floor of the original exhibition building of the Military Museum of the Chinese People's Revolution. It mainly shows that from 1945 to 1949, under the leadership of the Communist Party of China, the Chinese people and the People's Liberation Army fought bloody battles to overthrow the rule of the Kuomintang regime. The National War of Liberation Museum covers an exhibition area of 1,650 square meters, with 1,375 pieces of cultural relics and 7 pieces of art

On the basis of the exhibition Our Teams Towards the Sun, which was held in commemoration of the 80th anniversary of the founding of the Chinese army, the Hall of Achievements in the Construction of National Defense and the Armed Forces of the People's Republic of China, was adjusted to the east third floor of the original exhibition building at the end of 2007. From October 2009 to December 2009, it was revised and adjusted again. It was reopened on January 1, 2010. In May 2012, it was closed due to the reinforcement and renovation project of the Military Museum Exhibition Building. More than 2800 pieces of cultural relics, pictures, works of art, waxworks and landscapes are displayed.

Historical Evolution

On May 13, 1960, the Armory Department of the General Logistics Department handed over 1,525 pieces of weapons, equipment and ammunition to the Military Museum of the Chinese People's Revolution.
In August 1965, the museum identified 936 first-grade cultural relics in its collection, including 229 for the Red Army, 206 for the War of Resistance against Japanese Aggression, 263 for the War of Liberation, 159 for the War of Resistance against American Aggression and Aid for Korea, 62 for the Preservation of Socialist Construction, and 17 for weapons.

On October 13, 1970, the military museum set up the leading organization according to the trial establishment, replacing the "reform committee". Zhou Chu was appointed political commissar, and Geng Xuewen was appointed deputy director. And set up a temporary Party committee. The museum has three subordinate departments: the political department, the management office and the display and publicity office.
