South Luogu Lane
Night Scene of South Luogu Lane

Introduction

As one of the oldest neighborhoods in Beijing, South Luogu Lane has a history of more than 740 years. It was named Arched Lane because of its high terrain in the middle and low in the north and south, like a hunchback.
Of course, the reason why South Luogu Lane is popular is not only because there are a large number of fresh and artistic shops here, which attract a lot of young people and tourists, but also because of the complete layout and rich history of this lane.

South Luogu Lane

Main scenes

South Luogu Lane is the only chessboard style traditional residential area in China that has completely preserved the courtyard texture of Yuan Dynasty Lane, the largest scale, the highest grade and the most abundant resources. It is also the street and lane with the most old Beijing style. All kinds of mansions and houses in the surrounding lanes are colorful, thick and profound.

The Maoer Lane in South Luogu Lane was named for the hat-making workshop in the Qing Dynasty. Maoer Lane was the residence of the last Empress Wanrong before her wedding, commonly known as the "Empress's Mansion."The most famous official office in history was the Yamen in the No. 45 Yard of Maoer Lane, which was called "North Yamen" by the old Beijingers.

Yuer Lane in South Luogu Lane was once the residence of Qi Baishi, a master of Chinese painting. The house was built in the Qing Dynasty and bought by the Ministry of Culture after liberation as the residence of Mr. Qi Baishi.The yard was not repainted, giving the original taste of the old house door.In the courtyard, there is a sculpture of Qi Baishi. In the main room, there are paintings, quilts and other old things used by the old man.

The Houyuanensi Lane in South Luogu Lane is the former residence of Conformity. Compared with many deep residences in this area, the former residence of Conformity is very insignificant. It is only a small two-entrance quadrangle. Mr.Mao Dun moved here in 1974 and spent the last seven years of his life in the small hospital until his death in 1981.

Here each Lane, each house carries the owner of their own joys and sorrows, different traces of history.Push the door and go, meditation feeling, every story here is worth exploring.

Tourism Value

Those who miss old Beijing like it here, because it has the most authentic Beijing flavor. From the Ming Dynasty generals to the Empress of the late Qing Dynasty, from the literary masters to the masters of the painting world, every house here is filled with countless romantic characters. Blue-eyed, white-skinned foreigners like it because it has their usual elegance and romance. Outside is the dust and smoke of old Chinese Lanes. Inside is the familiar Blue Mountain coffee and gin.

You seem to be wandering in the time between ancient and modern, feeling the passionate collision between tradition and fashion in the space of combining Chinese and Western culture. It's so contradictory, yet incredibly harmonious.
People can not help but sigh, the original Beijing can be so leisurely.
